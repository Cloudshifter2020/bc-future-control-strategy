01 — The problem, widened

The reconciliation gap is a symptom, not the disease.

The original brief was marketing spend reconciliation across the property sales process. That is a real and worthwhile problem — but it sits inside a larger pattern worth naming before solving.

Marketing is arranged operationally, supplier invoices land in finance, and the reconciliation between what was expected, approved, and invoiced happens manually and late. Closing that single gap is valuable. But the same disconnect repeats across the finance function, and a system chosen to fix only the marketing problem will leave the rest untouched.

Solve the marketing reconciliation in isolation and you have a better spreadsheet. Solve the operating model and you have a finance function that scales.
01

Operational-to-finance handoffs

Marketing committed in MRI, invoices received in finance, with no shared reference holding the two together until someone reconciles by hand.

02

Accounts payable processing

Every supplier invoice is opened, read, coded, routed, and matched manually — effort that scales linearly with volume and property count.

03

Bank reconciliation & period close

Month-end reconciliation and variance commentary remain a manual exercise, compressing the team's time precisely when reporting deadlines bite.

04

Forecasting across disconnected tools

Sales pipeline lives in the operational system, commissions in spreadsheets, cash flow somewhere else — no single forward view of the business.

Business Central as the financial core, the Power Platform extending it, and AI layered onto every tier — not bolted on at the end, but built into how each layer works.

AI The principle that holds it together

AI without a clean data foundation is theatre.

Every agent in this design depends on the tier beneath it being in place first. That is why this is sequenced as a roadmap, not a switch — each phase delivers value on its own and lays the data foundation the next phase's intelligence needs.

03 — The roadmap

04 — Why this holds up to scrutiny

Four things a finance reviewer will want to be true.

Controls are native

The internal controls and audit trail are part of the ERP itself, not a workaround layered on top. That distinction matters most to the people who sign the accounts.

Value at every phase

No phase depends on the next one to justify itself. The roadmap can pause after any stage and still leave the business better off than it started.

One data model

Dynamics 365 and the Power Platform share Dataverse. Reporting reads from the same ledger the team works in — no duplication, no overnight exports, no silos.

AI earns its place

Each AI capability is tied to a specific, measurable task — invoice capture, reconciliation, forecasting — rather than added as a headline with no job to do.
